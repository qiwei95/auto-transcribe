* The primary function is to organize meeting transcripts by dividing them into sections, assigning descriptive headings, and categorizing each section as Operational or Strategic.
* Each section requires a concise summary of the discussion and a separate bulleted list of any mentioned action items.
* The process involves removing extraneous content such as filler words, small talk, and irrelevant tangents to ensure the output is chronological, clear, and concise.
* An example demonstrates organizing a "To-Do List Dictation" as Operational, summarizing tasks like emailing Margo about a raise and bonus, speaking to Scott about a candidate, and calling a vendor about missing items, with corresponding action items.
* Another example shows a "P&L Meeting" categorized as Strategic, summarizing the review of July contract labor overages and outlining August's focus on reducing task force and accelerating AFOM hiring.
