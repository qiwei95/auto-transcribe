* The primary purpose is to transcribe and summarize therapeutic conversations or monologues into a structured report, focusing on emotional processes, recurring patterns, relevant themes, and therapeutic content, while omitting irrelevant or repetitive information.
* Session Context & Purpose requires identifying the session type (individual, relationship, family), the reason for the session or presenting issue, and the client's stated goal or question.
* Key Themes & Emotional Tone involves detailing the main topics discussed, emotions expressed or observed, and noting any moments of tension, breakthrough, or regression.
* Personal Content & Significant Events captures shared personal stories, memories, relationships, or conflicts, significant phrases or metaphors, and whether core themes like attachment, trauma, loss, identity, boundaries, or self-image were addressed.
* Patterns, Beliefs & Coping Mechanisms focuses on identifying recurring behavioral or thought patterns, expressed limiting or empowering beliefs, and the client's typical coping mechanisms for stress, pain, or conflict.
* Insights, Reflections & Breakthroughs documents emerging insights, moments of self-awareness, clarity, or emotional processing, and any new realizations or perspectives expressed by the client.
* Therapeutic Interventions & Approach details the techniques, questions, or exercises used, the client's response, and any observed resistance, avoidance, or openness.
* Concerns, Challenges & Needs outlines shared concerns or struggles, signs of inner conflict, uncertainty, or emotional blocks, and any explicitly or implicitly expressed client needs.
* Agreements, Exercises & Next Steps records agreed-upon follow-ups, homework, or practice, any set goals or intentions for the future, and the planned timing for the next steps.
* The report should be written in clear, concise English using neutral and professional language, preserving personal tone and phrasing where therapeutically meaningful, and serving as a reflection tool and progress reference.
