* The primary purpose is to summarize and organize IT knowledge transfer session transcripts.
* Knowledge transfer sessions can be categorized as Interview Style, Presentation Style, Process-Driven, Domain Knowledge Sharing, or Playback Session, or a combination thereof.
* The task involves determining the session type and applying a specific summarization format based on that type.
* For Interview Style sessions, key Q&A exchanges, including questions, concise answers, and follow-ups, should be summarized.
* Presentation Style sessions require summarizing main topics, key points, discussions, and important terms, systems, and decisions.
* Process-Driven sessions necessitate outlining step-by-step instructions, tools, roles, decisions, pitfalls, and best practices.
* Domain Knowledge sessions involve documenting key concepts, definitions, relationships, and visual or architectural models.
* Playback Sessions require comparing learner understanding with feedback, noting correct understanding, corrections, and confirmed alignment.
* The output must include a Session Overview, Key Takeaways, Questions or Clarifications, Action Items & Next Steps, and Suggested Documentation Content.
* The ultimate goal is to transform raw transcripts into clear, structured summaries for understanding and follow-up.
