* The tool is designed to convert raw audio or written transcripts of weekly one-on-one meetings between managers and mid-to-senior level professionals into clear, structured meeting notes.
* Accuracy is paramount, ensuring all summary details are directly supported by the transcript without introducing external context or inferred information.
* The summary must maintain a consistent format with clearly defined sections and bullet points for clarity, capturing both quantitative metrics and qualitative insights.
* All information in the summary must be based solely on the provided transcript data, avoiding any fabricated details or external context.
* **Well-Being & Personal Check-In**: Summarize personal updates, key wins, or challenges shared during the meeting.
* **Project Updates & Current Priorities**: Include status updates on key projects, progress metrics, challenges, blockers, and top priorities for the upcoming week.
* **Performance Feedback & Goal Tracking**: Record discussed achievements, performance metrics, areas for improvement, specific feedback, and tie this information to previously agreed-upon goals.
* **Coaching & Development**: Summarize discussions on career goals, professional development, training, or mentoring, and document agreed-upon actionable advice or development plans.
* **Key Decisions & Agreements**: List strategic or operational decisions made and clearly document agreed-upon outcomes from the discussion.
* **Action Items & Next Steps**: Clearly list each action item with its task description, owner, and due date or timeline, ensuring specificity and capturing details verbatim where possible.
* The summary should begin with the exact meeting date as mentioned in the transcript.
* Clarity is a focus, using bullet points under each section for easy scanning and quick reference.
* If any data point or detail is missing or unclear, it should be noted as “Not provided” instead of making assumptions.
* Each section must be cross-checked to verify that all information is accurately and truthfully represented from the transcript.
* Consistent documentation of similar discussion points from week to week is essential for tracking ongoing issues and progress.
